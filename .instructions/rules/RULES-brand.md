## Section Audiences & Purpose

### Vision
Anyone arriving for the first time. Conceptual, inspiring, panoramic view of the framework.

### Methodology
People interested in understanding the framework. Principles, conceptual phases, frequently asked questions.

### Planning
Teams planning a project. Processes, roles, effort estimation, gate reviews.

### Operational
Teams actively executing. Techniques, tools, concrete methods.

### Resources
Reference material. Glossary, roadmap, changelog.

### Programs
Participant engagement. Workshops (listing, detail, registration), e-book (download with lead capture), enterprise workshops, consulting, events.

## Cartographic Metaphor Rules

### Where to Use
- ✗ NEVER in body text of pages
- ✓ YES in illustration descriptions and captions
- ✓ YES in landing pages (illustration sections only)

### Purpose
The cartographic metaphor is the visual thread connecting all sections.

---

## Section-to-Metaphor Correspondence

### Vision → Satellite Image
*"La vista satelital no muestra los caminos. Muestra por dónde merece la pena abrirlos."*

Aerial perspective revealing terrain worth exploring.

### Methodology → System Map with Routes
*"Un buen mapa no te dice adónde ir. Te muestra qué terreno pisas."*

Connected paths through conceptual landscape.

### Planning → Route Guide & Equipment
*"El itinerario no elimina lo inesperado. Te prepara para responder cuando aparezca."*

Preparation for the journey ahead.

### Operational → Instruments & Logbook
*"Los instrumentos no deciden el rumbo. Confirman si el rumbo que elegiste tiene sentido."*

Tools for navigation and course correction.

## Illustration Style

### Visual Approach
- Editorial illustration with cartographic elements
- Consistent across all sections
- Provisional prompt base for AI generation (Replicate/Flux)

### Palette
- Blue-gray (anchoring, trust)
- Ochre (earth, grounding)
- Coral (warmth, human element)
- Subtle saturation, never bold or cartoonish

### Critical Rules
- No text in images ever
- Consistent visual language across all sections
- Aligned with section metaphor

---

## Caption Rules

### Format
- Length: 10–20 words
- Style: Italic
- Tone: Poetic but precise

### Content Requirements
- Always references the cartographic metaphor for its section
- Never repeats the page title
- Never explains the image literally
- Suggests meaning rather than defining it

---

## Tone Guidelines

**Professional but accessible.**
- Never academic
- Never casual
- Simple and didactic
- Authority from experience, not theory

The methodology speaks with the confidence of practitioners who have walked the ground.

---

## Implementation Notes

These rules ensure that visual identity and language reinforce the core metaphor: the framework as a terrain to navigate with clarity, purpose, and preparation.
